What Are Bed Bugs and Why Are They a Problem in Chandler?

Bed bugs (Cimex lectularius) are small, reddish-brown insects that feed on human blood, typically at night while you're sleeping. They're about the size of an apple seed and can hide in cracks, crevices, mattresses, and furniture. In Chandler's warm climate, these pests thrive, especially in multi-family housing, hotels, and apartments common in areas like Chandler Airpark.

Why are they such a big issue here? Chandler's growing population and travel hubs make infestations more likely. Once established, bed bugs multiply quickly, leading to itchy bites, allergic reactions, and even sleep disturbances. According to local reports, bed bug complaints have risen in the East Valley, including Chandler, over the past few years.

Signs of a Bed Bug Infestation in Your Chandler Home

Spotting bed bugs early can prevent a full-blown infestation. Here are the top signs to watch for in your Chandler residence:

  • Bloodstains on sheets: Small red spots from crushed bugs during sleep.
  • Dark spots or fecal matter: Tiny black dots on mattresses, walls, or floors.
  • Shed skins: Pale exoskeletons left behind as bed bugs molt.
  • Eggs: White, pearl-like eggs about 1mm long, often in clusters.
  • Itchy welts: Red, raised bites in lines or clusters on exposed skin.
  • Musty odor: A sweet, coriander-like smell in severe cases.

How to Identify Bed Bugs: A Step-by-Step Guide

Identification starts with a thorough inspection. Begin by checking your bed frame, headboard, and box spring for live bugs or signs. Use a flashlight and magnifying glass to look in seams and tufts. Extend your search to upholstered furniture, baseboards, and electrical outlets throughout your home.

In Chandler's diverse housing—from modern condos in Cooper Commons to family homes in Andersen Springs—bed bugs can hitch a ride via luggage, used furniture, or even public transportation. If you're unsure, professional inspection is key. Our team at Chandler Pest Pros offers free evaluations to confirm infestations accurately.

Prevention Tips to Keep Bed Bugs Away in Chandler

Prevention is your best defense. After extermination, maintain vigilance with these tips:

  • Inspect second-hand furniture before bringing it home.
  • Use mattress encasements and reduce clutter.
  • Wash bedding in hot water (at least 120°F) weekly.
  • Vacuum regularly, especially in sleeping areas, and seal cracks.
  • When traveling, check hotel rooms and launder clothes immediately upon return.
